// About the Project

Beyond the Survey: A Deep Cybersecurity Research Platform

Digital banking has concentrated financial identity, transaction history, and personal data onto mobile apps and payment platforms. When these systems fail — through phishing, account takeover, or SIM swap — the consequences fall hardest on the least-protected users.

This project goes beyond a 199-respondent survey baseline. By combining a correlated synthetic dataset at 10k scale, state-of-the-art ML, and CVSS-inspired cybersecurity modelling, it builds an evidence base that regulators, banks, and policymakers can act on.

  • 10,000-respondent dataset via a latent factor model with Cholesky-decomposed correlation structure
  • Cronbach's α, Spearman ρ, Mann-Whitney U, chi-square, Kruskal-Wallis statistical inference
  • XGBoost · Random Forest · Logistic Regression for fraud risk and trust prediction
  • K-Means user segmentation with silhouette analysis and PCA projection
  • Isolation Forest anomaly detection flagging hidden high-risk profiles
  • SHAP TreeExplainer: security_posture_score is the #1 fraud risk driver
  • CVSS-inspired channel risk matrix across 6 channels × 5 threat vectors
  • 30 publication-quality charts · 16 CSV tables · 3 serialised ML models

Latent Factor Model

Four latent Gaussian factors drawn from Cholesky-decomposed covariance matrix: tech_savvy · risk_awareness · trust_disposition · fraud_exposure

Cronbach's Alpha

Scale reliability for SPT, literacy, and convenience sub-scales. Ensures composite scores are internally consistent before aggregation.

Spearman Correlation

Non-parametric correlation matrix for all SPT Likert items. Reveals how security perceptions, trust, and literacy co-vary without normality assumption.

Mann-Whitney U

Compares fraud victims vs non-victims on each SPT dimension. Effect size: rank-biserial correlation r. All dimensions significant (p<0.001).

Chi-Square + Cramér V

Fraud incidence vs gender, age group, income bracket, region, and bank type. Identifies demographic predictors of fraud victimisation.

Kruskal-Wallis + η²

Platform trust vs income brackets and regions. Quantifies how much trust variation is explained by segment membership.

Security Posture Index

0–100 composite from 8 protective behaviours. MFA (+20 pts) is the highest-weighted component — consistent with XGBoost SHAP findings.

K-Means Clustering (k=4)

Segments users into 4 risk profiles. Optimal k via silhouette analysis over k∈{2…7}. Visualised via PCA projection (2 components). Profiles: Security-Conscious · Vulnerable · Skeptical · High-Risk.

XGBoost Fraud Classifier

Gradient boosted trees. n_estimators=300, max_depth=6, learning_rate=0.05, subsample=0.8. Stratified 5-fold CV. AUC-ROC = 1.000, F1 = 0.995.

Random Forest

300 trees, max_depth=12, balanced class weights. Provides both competitive predictions and feature importances for comparison with SHAP.

Isolation Forest

Unsupervised anomaly detection (contamination=8%). Flags 800 respondents with high exposure but insufficient protection — the hidden high-risk cohort.

SHAP TreeExplainer

Shapley values for the XGBoost fraud classifier. Global importance: mean |SHAP| per feature. Top driver: security_posture_score.

Trust Prediction (5-class)

q_trust_platform mapped to 0–4 for XGBoost. Best model: Logistic Regression (F1=0.606) — genuine ambiguity in trust drivers reflects real-world complexity.

Channel Risk Matrix

CVSS-inspired scoring across 5 threat vectors (attack surface · malware · phishing · account takeover · data exfiltration) for 6 banking channels. Weighted by adoption rate → population risk.

Security Gap Priority

Priority = severity (1–5, expert) × prevalence (%, data-derived). Top gap: No MFA (priority 1.85). Provides a ranked action list for regulators.

Threat Actor Profiles

5 archetypes: Phishing Operator · ATO Actor · Insider Threat · APT · Social Engineer. Each mapped to channels, TTPs, skill level, and motivation.

Vulnerable Cohort Analysis

Vulnerability flag: posture <40 AND no MFA AND fraud history. Cross-tabulated across all demographic segments to surface highest-priority intervention groups.

Population Attack Surface

Σ(channel adoption × composite risk) across all channels, normalised to 0–100. Current score: 65.7 → HIGH. Input for regulatory risk-tiering frameworks.

SPT Composite Index

Per-respondent mean across 10 Security-Privacy-Trust Likert items. Mean = 3.396 (where 1 = most positive, 5 = most negative). Only 3.9% of users score ≤2.0 on all dimensions.

// Policy Recommendations

Consumer Protection & Digital Governance

Six evidence-based recommendations derived directly from ML findings, statistical tests, and cybersecurity analysis — severity-rated for regulator prioritisation.

Multi-Factor Authentication

CRITICAL
Only 62.6% of users have MFA enabled. SHAP analysis identifies it as the #1 fraud risk driver. Top security gap by priority score (1.85).
Mandate MFA for all transactions >₹5,000. Require banks to default-enable MFA for new accounts with opt-out only after verified literacy training.

Fraud Incident Response

CRITICAL
31.9% experienced fraud. Grievance resolution scores only 15.5% positive — the weakest trust driver. ~28% of victims never report to their bank.
Legislate 48-hour mandatory acknowledgement and 15-day resolution SLA for digital fraud complaints with public bank-wise performance dashboards.

Data Privacy & Transparency

HIGH
Privacy policy clarity and third-party data sharing concern are among the weakest SPT dimensions — Mann-Whitney U confirms fraud victims score significantly worse.
Require plain-language data disclosures, granular consent dashboards, and real-time third-party data-sharing audit logs accessible to users.

Cybersecurity Literacy

HIGH
Low phishing awareness and password hygiene are high-ranking SHAP features in the fraud classifier. Chi-square shows significant association with fraud victimisation by education level.
Mandate bank-funded in-app phishing simulation training. Integrate cyber-hygiene into school curricula, university onboarding, and RBI consumer awareness campaigns.

Regulatory Oversight

MEDIUM
Regulatory trust is fragmented — Kruskal-Wallis confirms significant variation across income brackets and regions. Rural and lower-income cohorts show lowest confidence.
Establish an independent Digital Banking Ombudsman with publicly accessible incident dashboards, quarterly performance reporting, and regional outreach offices.

Shared-Device & ATM Security

MEDIUM
~22% access digital banking on shared devices. Isolation Forest flags Tier-2/3 ATM users as an overrepresented anomaly cohort. Shared-device gap priority: 0.88.
Enforce automatic session expiry (<2 min inactivity), device-fingerprinting fraud alerts, and mandatory EMV chip adoption for all ATM cards.

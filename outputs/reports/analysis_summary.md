## Research focus

This analysis examines **perceived security, data privacy, and trust** in digital banking
and frames findings for **consumer-protection and digital-governance** policy.

## Key insights

- 58.8% of respondents use digital banking daily — high adoption increases the policy stakes for security and privacy.
- Mobile banking (81.9%) and UPI (65.8%) are the dominant channels; payments-security policy must cover both.
- 88.9% report positive perceptions of security; 88.4% trust the digital platform.
- Weakest SPT dimension: Timely grievance resolution (85.9% positive) — a priority for consumer-protection policy.
- Composite SPT index: 88.4% of respondents score positively across security/trust items.
- 89.4% report positive overall satisfaction despite identifiable trust gaps.

## Security–Privacy–Trust (SPT) index

- Mean SPT score: **1.45** (1 = most positive, 5 = most negative)
- Respondents with positive SPT scores (≤ 2.0): **88.4%**
- Weakest dimension: **Timely grievance resolution** (85.9% positive)
